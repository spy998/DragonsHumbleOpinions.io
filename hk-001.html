TRANSCRIPT

The City of Tears

The constant trickle of water.

That's the only sound that remains in this place.

Well, and the grunts of the reanimated guards that roam these levels.

Zote said their devotion is foolish.

While it's easy to think that as they fall to my nail because preventing entry from outsiders is all they know; I can't help but wonder if he's wrong.

If he is not, perhaps I am no better than them. Something is driving me forward, to explore every nook of this place, to notate the barriers in my way and return when I am able to demolish them.

Is that just an explorers spirit within me that causes this?

Like the bright spirited Cornifer do I do this because of a love for it that wells within me?

I think, and fear, that is not quite it...

I think I will sit and listen to the rain for a while. These thoughts are heavy, but the sound of water against the glass makes things lighter.
